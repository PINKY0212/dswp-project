Understanding the Problem

Every hour, Alex must decide how much electricity should be produced and scheduled for the next 30–60 minutes. These decisions keep millions of homes, hospitals, and industries running safely.

The Core Challenge

As outlined in our project proposal, electricity demand is no longer driven by simple,
linear patterns. It is shaped by complex, non-linear interactions between:

  • Strong seasonal structure (hourly, daily, weekly)
  • Rapid weather changes (temperature, wind, solar)
  • Human behavior and calendar effects

No single forecasting model consistently balances accuracy, robustness during peaks, and interpretability for real-time grid operations.

The modeling dilemma Alex faces

Statistical models (SARIMA)

Trusted, interpretable, and stable. But they struggle when demand reacts sharply to sudden weather or behavioral shifts.

Deep learning models (LSTM)

Powerful at capturing non-linear interactions. But they behave like black boxes — difficult to trust in high-stakes grid decisions.

This project investigates whether hybrid and ensemble approaches can combine the strengths of both worlds.

Alex is finding answers to these Research Questions

  1. Accuracy: How do SARIMA and LSTM models compare for short-term (30–60 min) load forecasting?
  2. Peak stability: Can hybrid SARIMA+LSTM or ensemble models reduce error variance during peak-load periods?
  3. Explainability: Do SHAP provide meaningful explanations for Different models predictions?
  4. Operational feasibility: Are these models computationally viable for real-time grid operation?
